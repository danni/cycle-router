Crowdsourced cycle maps

Every day lots of people are recording the rides on their GPSes and uploading it to the web. Let's use this data to create crowd sourced cycle maps for Melbourne (and other cities).

How?

By clicking the button below we will take you to RunKeeper and ask you to authorize Cycle Router. This will then add you to our database to periodically synchronise your cycling data and add it to our database.

We will then count the number of people cycling at a location against data from Open Streetmap. We will also consider velocity and time to try and work out what time what routes are best. This part is very much a work in progress, and requires more data before it can be useful.

Let's go!

Why?

Because there should be better cycling maps.

What about privacy?

A record of your RunKeeper account, and a complete set of your tracks will be stored in our database. The database does not contain any personal details, but someone could use your user id to look at anything on your profile. Because your tracks will frequently start or end at your home, school or workplace, we are going to strip that data out the maps we generate, but it is in the database.

If you wish to revoke our access you can do so from the RunKeeper apps page. Doing this will not remove you from our database. If you want to do so, you should wait until Danni implements it.

What about Strava?

I am open to adding other cycle tracking services, e.g. Strava. The only requirement is they have an API we can access data from (currently Strava does not).
